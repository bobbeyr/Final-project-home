Transportation Methods

CoGo Bikeshares Central Ohio Bikeways Cota Bus Services

This is a list of Columbus's different modes of transportation.
There aren't to many but there are also different types of modes like Ubers.
The Urber foundation varies depending on the state. Some states have you pay by the mile
others pay by the minute. These are ways to travel around Columbus if you don't have your own car.

CoGo Bikeshares: How CoGo Bikeshares works is pretty simple. You download
the app that is prompted. When you find one of the bikes just scan the QR code that is
attached to the bike by using the app's QR code scanner. You will then be able to cruise around the city.
When you finish riding around you can go to a CoGo docking station. Once the lights turn green your good to dock.

Central Ohio Bikeways: This way of transportation is also very simple,
There are over 230 miles of different trails that connects Ohio. You can go on these
trails by Bike, Scooter, Skateboard, and even running or walking. This is a lot more easy because
this mode of transportation involves your own speed and less money. You don't always have to rush as if someone
is taking you because you can freely move at your own speed. So why not give it a shot.

Cota Bus Services: To ride a Cota bus you want to download the transit app.
With this downloaded you can plan your next trip and find the closest bus station in
your area. You can also check the prices and different destinations you could travel to. After
that you should make sure to get to a bus station at least 5 minutes early. When the bus arrives you
should make sure that the bus has the right destination on the top of the windsheild. If that is the case then
you will go ahead and board the Cota bus. You will pay uppon boarding. Then enjoy your ride and make sure to
get off at the right destination.
